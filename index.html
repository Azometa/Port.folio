Building Websites with AI

Using AI helped me move faster from “blank page” to a real website structure. The biggest benefit was getting quick layout ideas and cleaner wording, but I still had to understand what each part did. I learned that the best results come when I’m specific about requirements (sections, styling, accessibility, and mobile behavior), then I test and adjust the code myself. It made me more confident because I could iterate quickly while still learning how HTML/CSS work under the hood.

Interview Question

Question: “Tell me about a time you solved a problem for someone.”

When someone had an issue and was frustrated, I stayed calm and asked a few clear questions to understand what was happening. I broke the problem down into steps, tried the quickest fixes first, and explained what I was doing in simple terms. Once it was resolved, I confirmed everything worked and shared what to do if it happens again. That experience reinforced that good support is both technical and communication — the person should feel helped, not talked down to.
